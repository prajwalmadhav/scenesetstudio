Logo Concepts

Four marks.
Choose your identity.

Concept A
The Frame Mark
Two overlapping camera frame rectangles — magenta leading, ghost trailing. Signals creative direction, viewpoint authority, and cinematic framing in a single wordmark.
★ Recommended
Concept B
The Signature S
Abstract S mark formed from a single fluid stroke — organic, cinematic. Italic serif wordmark reinforces the storytelling/editorial personality of the brand.
Concept C
The Hexagon Monogram
SSS initials in a hexagonal container — signals precision, structure, and craft. Works at very small sizes (favicon, watermark, emboss on print).
Concept D
The Pure Wordmark
Weight contrast between heavy serif "Scene" and spaced-out display "SET STUDIO" — creates extreme visual hierarchy. Maximum flexibility, works at any scale, no icon dependency.

CTA Psychology

Buttons that trigger
decisions.

Stop Looking Like Everyone Else

Humans are 2× more motivated to escape pain than gain reward. This CTA implies the status quo is already costing them — invisibility is the threat.

Use: Hero section, above the fold

Visualize My Brand's Potential

The word "My" creates psychological ownership before clicking. "Visualize" triggers right-brain preview of a superior future — the brain begins imagining the outcome before committing.

Use: Primary CTA, single most important action

Create Revenue That Wasn't There

Strips all creative abstraction. Speaks only in business outcomes — no mention of "content" or "strategy." Ideal for commercial clients and performance-first buyers.

Use: Services page, commercial sections

See What Your Brand Could Feel Like

Opens a neurological loop the brain urgently wants to close. "Feel" bypasses rational comparison-shopping and activates emotional processing — the same pathway that makes great films unforgettable.

Use: Portfolio, case studies, mid-page
